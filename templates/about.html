The Prince George's County Health Department responds to inquiries concerning any licensed or unlicensed food service facilities or operations as a part of its Food Protection/Policy Program.



Facilities liable to routine inspections include restaurants, grocery stores, catering facilities, theater and stadium concessions, public and private schools, penal institutions, shelters and senior feeding programs; inspections for temporary or seasonal food operations including vending locations, carnivals, fairs, festivals and farmers markets; and inspections for mobile food units. These include both licensed or unlicensed food service facilities.



The county shows comprehensive, monitoring, and complaint investigations within the publicly available data.



Comprehensive inspections  are thorough evaluation of the sanitation, maintenance, and food handling for the facility.



Monitoring inspections  focus on critical food temperatures, equipment temperatures, general food handling and cleanliness practices.



Reinspections  are conducted to determine compliance with non-critical violations that require time to correct and to confirm that the correction of previously cited critical violations has been maintained.



Closure  indicates the temporary suspension of the food facility license due to an immediate health hazard that cannot be corrected immediately. This can also be the result of failure to correct a non-critical violation within the specified time period, failure to comply with health department requirements, and/or operating without a health department permit.
